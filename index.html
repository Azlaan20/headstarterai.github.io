About

I am an M.Sc. Electrical Engineering student at the University of Stuttgart, specializing in Machine Learning and Signal Processing within Smart Information Processing. My work sits at the intersection of AI, signal processing, embedded systems, biomedical engineering, and industrial automation.

I completed my B.E. Electrical Engineering from NUST College of EME with a final CGPA of 3.16 and degree award date of 06 June 2025. My academic and project work includes ECG acquisition and filtering, embedded systems, computer vision, FPGA-based design, power electronics, control systems, RF/antenna work, and applied machine learning.

Professionally, I worked as a Graduate Engineering Trainee at Engro Polymer & Chemicals Ltd. in electrical utilities, protection systems, automation, commissioning, and operational readiness. I also completed a funded A*STAR SIPGA research attachment at the Bioinformatics Institute in Singapore, where I worked with biomedical data workflows, Python, Docker, Linux, imaging tools, and machine-learning-oriented data preparation.

My current focus is building intelligent engineering systems for real-world applications: biomedical signal processing, industrial AI, embedded intelligence, automation analytics, and reliable data-driven engineering workflows.
